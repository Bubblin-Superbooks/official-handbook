And hyperlinking a few loose pages or even pagination in the traditional sense isn't the same thing. Spine restricts the arrangement on the stack and makes for linear reading experience. I found it particularly nice to read piecemealed content on mobile and tablets with page-flip. The spine broke down the content into small chewable chunks that were easier to remember.

About ten years ago people tried emulating the book with third-party plugins like Macromedia Flash. But those attempts were rather crude and experience far from ideal. Especially, with a desktop-only web all over. Flash undid the cause of books more than anything else. It left a bad aftertaste, so much so that many chose to ripping their books apart and converting them into websites. Thus ending up with bad design and often poor readership.

I wish web had better support for books with a spine_url to host and thread pages on. A tag that considered all semantics and pragmatics of a book and its subresources. Like pages, cover, colophon etc. Only then could web catch up with what's truly a book reading experience. Go beyond the scroll troll. But that's a discussion for another place and time.
