Concept

Unlike e-book, a physical book is an easy product to imagine. A hard spine with a stack of pages that hold the story together. The story itself flows through a 'turn of pages', an experience that also doubles up as a "memory-palace" building exercise. These important design principles of a physical book are essential ingredients of Superbooks too.

This is how a Superbook works:

A bifolium design that compiles into a responsive book on web.

A leaf, for example, has two sides—front and a back—so a book with say 15 leaves will have 30 pages to read.

A Superbook always has an even number of pages.
