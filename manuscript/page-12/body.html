Use VCS like git or svn to version and save all history of your work and co-author with any number of friends — designers, content writers or developers — anywhere in the world. Cash in on pull_requests with rapid diff'ing and discussion. Seek algorithmic help on code quality and perform automated tests. Push to deploy with Capistrano. Ha!

Compare that with what book authors have.

Microsoft Word?☺. The savvier ones probably have a little more than that — latex, markdown and lout — but the tooling pretty much ends there with stripped down features like boldface or italics and rather weak formatting due to the requirement of reflow (B.T.W did you know that reflow is no longer necessary for ebooks?). A little bit of inline images too but even those tend to float away with reflow leaving little for book makers to rely on.

The other difference between books and apps is that books, especially the physical ones, have a rigid spine that holds the pages (views) together. In a specific order or stack. Sevearal native apps emulating a book like the Kindle App and iBooks seem to have picked up this feature correctly but the web is lagging.
