Preface

On the surface, book writing may seem very different from app development — they are different trades after all — but at the heart of each, there is more similarity between the two than differences. For authors it is ultimately about great storytelling, impeccable prose, seductive design and airtight packaging. A plot to come out with a winner.

Compare what a book writer does, how she progresses into her story, how she manages edits after edits of her manuscript — how a deadline must always be met — with what app developers generally do. You're in for a surprise already I'd say. A chance to look at how one trade could benefit from the other.

Book writing requires a masterful use of grammatical constructs and subtle nuances of a human language (like English). Writing software too requires exactly the same pursuit only from an author who writes in computer language. Given that higher level computer languages vie for human-ness, convergance of the two trades eventually seems imminent. Both books and apps have a design need. Thankfully both treat design and content separately.
