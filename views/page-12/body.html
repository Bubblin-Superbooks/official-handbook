Use VCS like git or svn to version and save all history of your work, co-author with any number of friends — designers, content writers or developers — anywhere in the world. Cash in on _pull_requests_ with rapid diff'ing and discussion. Seek algorithmic help on writing style, grammar and punctuation. Perform automated tests and then push to deploy with Capistrano or Chef. Ha!

Compare that with what book authors have. Microsoft Word?☺. The savvier ones probably have a little more than that — latex, markdown and lout — but the tooling pretty much ends there with stripped down features limited to boldface, italics and rather weak formatting due to the requirement of reflow. By the way did you know that reflow is no longer necessary for e-books? A little bit of inline images but even those tend to float away with reflow leaving little control with book authors.

Another difference between books and apps is that books have a natural rigid spine that holds the pages (views) together. In a specific order or stack. It's not the same thing as hyperlinking a few loose webpages or even pagination in the traditional sense of web.
