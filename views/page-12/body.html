I wish web had native support for books with a spine_url. A tag (like the video tag) that considers all semantics and pragmatics of a book {:object} and its subresources. Only then could tooling for books match with what developers have for apps today. But this is a discussion for another place and time.

Let's now look at the third basic difference between books and apps — the database. It seems unlikely that books are going to need a database to back 'em up. For there is more similarity between books and static html websites than between books and dynamic apps with a database.

But there is certainly a possibility. If we were to consider workbooks where a few pages (ajaxified iframes) have forms to accept input from the readers — voila!, there we have a case for a database.
