Web seems to be lagging because the experience of spine isn't supported (proposed) yet and hyperlinking a few loose webpages or even pagination in the traditional sense of web isn't the same thing. Spine restricts the arrangement in a linear read order and has a significant influence on the reading experience itself. I found it particularly advantageous on mobile phones and tablets where piecemealing the context into small chewable chunks was very easy to achieve.

About ten years ago people tried emulating the spine with third-party plugins like Macromedia Flash but those crude attempts seem to have done more damage to the cause of books than good. I personally know of many developers who conceded to ripping apart their books at the spine and turning them into websites. Often ending up with bad design and poor readership.

I wish web had native support for books with spine_url and pages. A tag that considered all semantics and pragmatics of a book {:parent} and its pages {:children}. Only then could tooling for books be built ground up and be matched with what developers have for writing apps today. But that's a discussion for another place and time.
