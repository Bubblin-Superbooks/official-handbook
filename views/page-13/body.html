Let's now look at the third basic difference between books and apps — the database. It seems unlikely that books are going to need a database to back 'em up. For there is more similarity between books and static html websites than between books and dynamic apps with a database.

Compare it with what book authors have. Microsoft word? The savvier ones probably have a little more to play with — latex, markdown or lout — but the tooling with most ends with boldface and italics only. A little bit of images but those too float away with reflow.

There isn't a way to add a canvas experiment or do a comics in pure CSS3, for example. There is no way for book authors to visualize, edit and test their product locally. Or escape the idea of fifty odd formats of lifeless downloadable artifacts that they need to provide over the web.
